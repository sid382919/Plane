Airbus Aircraft

Airbus A220

Type: Narrow-body | Passengers: 100–150

The A220 is designed for short to medium routes. It is very fuel efficient and popular with modern airlines.

Airbus A320 Family (A318, A319, A320, A321)

Type: Narrow-body | Passengers: 120–220

The A320 family is one of the most successful aircraft families ever. It was the first to use full fly-by-wire controls.

Airbus A330

Type: Wide-body | Passengers: 250–300

The A330 is a long-haul aircraft used for international flights. It is known for reliability and comfort.

Airbus A350

Type: Wide-body | Passengers: 300–410

The A350 is a next-generation aircraft made with composite materials, offering excellent fuel efficiency and passenger comfort.

Airbus A380

Type: Superjumbo | Passengers: 500–850

The A380 is the largest passenger aircraft ever built, featuring two full passenger decks.

Boeing Aircraft

Boeing 737 Family

Type: Narrow-body | Passengers: 130–215

The Boeing 737 is the most produced jet airliner in history. It is mainly used for short and medium-haul routes.

Boeing 747

Type: Wide-body | Passengers: 400–600

Known as the “Queen of the Skies,” the Boeing 747 revolutionized long-distance air travel.

Boeing 767

Type: Wide-body | Passengers: 200–300

The 767 is commonly used for long-haul and cargo operations.

Boeing 777

Type: Wide-body | Passengers: 300–400

The Boeing 777 is one of the most successful long-haul aircraft, known for range and reliability.

Boeing 787 Dreamliner

Type: Wide-body | Passengers: 240–330

Built with advanced composite materials, the 787 offers better fuel efficiency and passenger comfort.

Basic Airplane Parts

Wings

Create lift to keep the airplane in the air.

Engines

Provide thrust to move the aircraft forward.

Fuselage

Main body carrying passengers and cargo.

Landing Gear

Supports takeoff, landing, and taxiing.

Tail

Provides stability and directional control.

Airlines

What Is an Airline?

An airline is a company that operates aircraft to transport passengers and cargo. Airlines own or lease airplanes and operate scheduled flights.

Major Airlines

  • American Airlines – United States
  • Delta Air Lines – United States
  • United Airlines – United States
  • Emirates – United Arab Emirates
  • Lufthansa – Germany
  • British Airways – United Kingdom
  • Qatar Airways – Qatar

Airlines choose aircraft based on route length, passenger demand, and operating costs.
